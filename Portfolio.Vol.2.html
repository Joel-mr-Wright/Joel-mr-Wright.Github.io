This portfolio showcases work created during my undergraduate at the University of Lincoln.



This portfolio showcases primarily academic work at University of Lincoln for my Postgraduate. Other work such as drawings and projects undertaken while and after my Post Graduate are located at the back of this document.

I have a high regard for traditional architecture and the use of hand drawn details and plans. Though it must be said that the University of Lincolnâ€™s School of Architecture focuses heavily on the contemporary. So much of my Post Graduate work is contemporary in its methodology and design.

In this portfolio the use of software such as AutoCAD, SketchUp, Photoshop and InDesign, Revit and Blender have been used. I also consider myself as a quick learner, most if not all the work done within this report has been self-taught.

During my time at the University of Lincoln I worked both as an individual and as a team. This was due to the university promoting collaborative projects throughout the year. This alongside the help from my lectures, Marcin Kolakowski (Design/ Theory), Peter Boldwin (Design), David Hall (Design and Technology), Deborah Whelan (Theory), Edward Farell (Professionalism) and Rosemary Fieldson (Professionalism), thanks to them I have a suitable knowledge of the construction profession. At the end of these five years of academic one can only learn so much whilst at university, only by working in the profession can one grow and develop the skills required for practice.

I thank and appreciate you for the time taken to look through this portfolio.
